# Cover Letter Template — English

## Usage Instructions (for Claude, not included in output)

This template is used to generate a cover letter for a job application.
The output must look like a natural, personal email from an individual developer.
DO NOT include any mention of a team, company, agency, or automated process.
Use placeholders {{...}} — they will be filled by the system or by the human during review.

## Template

Subject: Application for {{vacancy_title}} — {{developer_name}}

---

Hi,

I came across your {{vacancy_title}} position and I'm very interested in this opportunity.

I'm a {{developer_role}} with {{developer_experience_years}}+ years of experience. {{developer_key_strength_sentence}}

What caught my attention about this role:
- {{reason_1_based_on_vacancy}}
- {{reason_2_based_on_vacancy}}

In my recent work, {{relevant_experience_paragraph — 2-3 sentences about projects/technologies that are most relevant to this specific vacancy}}.

I'm comfortable working remotely and have experience collaborating with distributed teams across different time zones.

I'd love to discuss how my experience could be a good fit for your team. I'm available for a call at your convenience.

Best regards,
{{developer_name}}

---

## Placeholders Reference

| Placeholder | Description | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| {{vacancy_title}} | Job title from the vacancy | Senior React Developer |
| {{developer_name}} | Developer's full name | John Smith |
| {{developer_role}} | Developer's general role | frontend developer / backend engineer |
| {{developer_experience_years}} | Years of experience | 5 |
| {{developer_key_strength_sentence}} | One sentence about main strength | I specialize in building complex React applications with TypeScript. |
| {{reason_1_based_on_vacancy}} | Why this specific vacancy is interesting | The focus on performance optimization aligns with my recent work |
| {{reason_2_based_on_vacancy}} | Another reason | I have experience with the tech stack mentioned |
| {{relevant_experience_paragraph}} | 2-3 sentences about relevant experience | In my last project, I built a real-time dashboard using React and WebSocket... |

## Tone Guidelines

- Professional but warm, not overly formal
- Confident but not arrogant
- Show genuine interest in the specific role (not generic)
- Keep it concise — 150-200 words max
- No buzzwords, no clichés like "passionate about technology"
- Sound like a real person, not a template
